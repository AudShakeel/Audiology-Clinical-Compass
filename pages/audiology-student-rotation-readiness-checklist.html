Audiology Student Rotation Readiness Checklist

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: This checklist helps student clinicians prepare for audiology rotations with safer habits, stronger documentation, and better patient-centered care.

Patient message: This is a student-facing checklist, not a patient handout. It is safe to print for students and preceptors.

Facts at a glance

  • Clinical care should combine evidence, patient goals, professional standards, and supervised judgment (ASHA, 2024) (APSO, 2021).
  • Pediatric care requires awareness of developmental, family, safety, and early intervention needs (JCIH, 2019) (American Academy of Audiology, 2020).
  • Students should follow site policy, supervisor instructions, privacy rules, and state or institutional requirements.

Before clinic

  • Review the patient age, referral question, prior results, devices, and relevant medical history.
  • Know which tests are expected and why each test is being done.
  • Prepare equipment, infection-control supplies, forms, and calibration or biological check routines according to site policy.

During the appointment

  • Introduce yourself as a supervised student.
  • Use plain language with patients and families.
  • Ask permission before touching devices, ears, or equipment.
  • Escalate red flags to the supervisor immediately.

Documentation habits

  • Record test conditions, reliability, transducers, masking, device settings, patient report, counseling, and follow-up plan.
  • Avoid unsupported claims and document clinical reasoning clearly.
  • Use outcome measures and official sources appropriately without copying restricted materials.

Professional habits

  • Arrive prepared and ask focused questions.
  • Receive feedback without defensiveness.
  • Know your limits and ask for help before patient safety or data quality is affected.
  • Protect patient privacy and never share identifiable information outside approved systems.

Take-home message

A strong student clinician is prepared, safe, humble, evidence-aware, and patient-centered.
