Audiology Student Rotation Readiness Checklist

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Students, externs, preceptors

Patient population: AuD students entering diagnostic, hearing aid, pediatric, CI, vestibular, or electrophysiology rotations

Clinical purpose: Improve readiness and clinic efficiency.

Key counseling points

  • Students should arrive prepared to explain test purpose, infection-control steps, patient instructions, equipment needs, common red flags, and documentation expectations for the rotation setting.
  • Before a hearing technology rotation, students should review otoscopy, audiogram interpretation, hearing aid verification, real-ear measures, troubleshooting, earmolds, batteries or charging, and patient counseling (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (Audiology Practice Standards Organization, 2021).
  • Before a pediatric or specialty rotation, students should review developmental test selection, cross-check principles, family-centered counseling, and referral pathways (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019) (American Academy of Audiology, 2020).

Clinician notes

Use during onboarding. Preceptors can adapt it into a site-specific competency checklist.

Red flags and referral indicators

Students must notify the preceptor immediately for medical red flags, safety concerns, equipment malfunction, unreliable results, or scope questions.

What happens next

Print for rotation orientation and ask student to identify three learning goals.
