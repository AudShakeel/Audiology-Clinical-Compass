Conductive, Sensorineural, and Mixed Hearing Loss

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Audiograms help show where the hearing problem may be occurring: outer or middle ear, inner ear or nerve, or a combination.

Patient message: Ask your audiologist to explain your air-conduction results, bone-conduction results, speech scores, and whether referral is recommended.

Facts at a glance

  • A conductive component means sound is not efficiently reaching the inner ear, often because of an outer or middle ear pathway problem (NICE, 2023) (FDA, 2022).
  • A sensorineural component means the inner ear, hearing nerve, or both are not encoding sound clearly (WHO, 2021) (ASHA, 2024).
  • Mixed hearing loss includes both conductive and sensorineural components, so management may require medical evaluation plus hearing technology (NICE, 2023) (FDA, 2022) .

Conductive hearing loss

  • This may be related to earwax, outer ear blockage, middle ear fluid, eardrum problems, ossicular problems, infection, or other conditions that require individualized evaluation (NICE, 2023) (FDA, 2022).
  • People may describe sound as plugged, muffled, or like listening through cotton.

Sensorineural hearing loss

  • This may be related to aging, noise exposure, genetics, ototoxicity, congenital factors, illness, or other inner-ear or neural causes (WHO, 2021) (Shearer et al., 2025).
  • People may describe speech as unclear, especially in noise, even when volume is increased.

Mixed hearing loss

  • Mixed hearing loss means both pathways are involved, so amplification may help but medical findings should also be reviewed when indicated (NICE, 2023) (FDA, 2022).
  • Treatment decisions depend on cause, stability, ear health, communication goals, and whether hearing aid, bone-conduction, or implantable options are appropriate.

When to get medical help

  • Seek prompt medical care for sudden hearing change, drainage, pain, dizziness, trauma, visible ear abnormality, or one-sided/asymmetric symptoms (FDA, 2022) (NICE, 2023).

Take-home message

The type of hearing loss helps guide the next step, but it is only one part of the full clinical picture.
