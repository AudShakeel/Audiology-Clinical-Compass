Vestibular Rehabilitation: What Patients Should Know

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Vestibular audiologists, PT partners, patients

Patient population: Patients referred for balance or dizziness rehabilitation

Clinical purpose: Explain rehabilitation goals and expectations.

Original patient counseling visual. It does not replace vestibular testing or medical evaluation.

Key counseling points

  • Vestibular rehabilitation is often used to improve gaze stability, balance, motion tolerance, and daily function when appropriate for the diagnosis (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2017) (Staab et al., 2017).
  • Exercises should be individualized by a trained clinician because the wrong activity, wrong dose, or missed red flag can worsen symptoms or delay needed medical care (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2017) (Staab et al., 2017).
  • Progress is usually monitored by symptom pattern, fall risk, gait, balance confidence, participation, and patient-specific goals (Jacobson and Newman, 1990) (Powell and Myers, 1995) (Wrisley et al., 2004) (Morris et al., 2009).

Clinician notes

Use when referring to vestibular PT. Audiologists can explain goals but should avoid prescribing PT protocols outside scope/training.

Red flags and referral indicators

Refer for neurologic signs, sudden hearing loss, new severe headache, drop attacks, fainting, or unsafe gait (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2017).

What happens next

Consider DHI, ABC, FGA, VAP, and fall-risk screening.
