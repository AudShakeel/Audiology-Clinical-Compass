NF2-Related Schwannomatosis

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: NF2-related schwannomatosis is a genetic tumor predisposition condition that can involve vestibular schwannomas and hearing, balance, tinnitus, or facial nerve concerns.

Patient message: This handout is not tumor treatment guidance. It is for hearing and balance counseling alongside specialty medical care.

Facts at a glance

  • Genetic conditions can affect hearing through neural, inner-ear, or broader medical mechanisms (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026).
  • Hearing aid and assistive technology decisions should be individualized based on hearing levels, speech understanding, ear status, and goals (ASHA, 2024) (FDA, 2022).
  • Medical management of tumors and imaging surveillance belongs with the specialty medical team (GeneReviews, 2026) .

Possible symptoms

  • Hearing loss in one or both ears.
  • Tinnitus.
  • Dizziness, imbalance, or falls.
  • Facial numbness, weakness, or other neurologic symptoms depending on tumor location.

Audiology care

  • Baseline and serial audiograms.
  • Speech understanding and word recognition monitoring.
  • Hearing technology discussion, which may include hearing aids, CROS/BiCROS, bone-conduction, cochlear implant, auditory brainstem implant, captions, or remote microphones depending on medical and audiologic factors.
  • Listening and communication support for school, work, and safety.

When to contact the medical team

  • Sudden hearing change.
  • New facial weakness, numbness, severe headache, neurologic symptoms, or worsening balance.
  • New inability to use a device because of sound distortion or discomfort.

Questions to ask

  • How should hearing be monitored with imaging and medical visits?
  • Which technology options are realistic for my hearing pathway?
  • What communication supports should I use now?

Take-home message

NF2-related care requires close coordination between audiology, ENT/neurotology, neurology/neurosurgery, genetics, and rehabilitation teams.
