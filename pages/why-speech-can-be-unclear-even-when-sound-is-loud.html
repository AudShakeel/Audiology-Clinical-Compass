Why Speech Can Be Unclear Even When Sound Is Loud

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Many people say, "I hear you, but I do not understand you." This can happen when the speech signal is loud enough but important speech details are not clear or are masked by background noise.

Patient message: Turning up volume may not solve speech clarity if the main issue is noise, distance, competing talkers, or reduced high-frequency speech detail.

Facts at a glance

  • Speech understanding is affected by audibility, inner-ear distortion, background noise, distance, reverberation, cognition, attention, and listening effort (ASHA, 2024) (ASHA, 2024).
  • Speech-in-noise testing can help document listening difficulty that may not be obvious from the audiogram alone (Etymotic Research, 2024) (ASHA, 2024).
  • Hearing aids can improve access to sound, but they cannot remove every effect of noise, distance, or room acoustics (FDA, 2022) (NICE, 2023) .

Why this happens

  • Consonants carry many speech details and are often softer and higher in pitch than vowels (ASHA, 2024).
  • Noise competes with speech and can hide important cues, especially when the talker is far away or not facing the listener (Etymotic Research, 2024) (ASHA, 2024).
  • Fast speech, group conversation, fatigue, and multitasking can increase listening effort (ASHA, 2024).

What can help

  • Verified hearing aid settings can improve audibility for speech while keeping loud sounds comfortable (ASHA, 2024) (Almufarrij et al., 2021).
  • Remote microphones can improve the speech signal by placing the microphone closer to the talker (American Academy of Audiology, 2008).
  • Captions, written keywords, quieter seating, and asking for a slower pace can reduce communication strain.

What to ask your audiologist

  • Would speech-in-noise testing help explain my difficulty?
  • Should my noise program, directional microphones, or remote microphone options be reviewed?
  • Are my devices verified for speech audibility and comfort?

When to get medical help

  • Sudden change in hearing, new one-sided tinnitus, ear pain, drainage, or dizziness should not be treated as a routine clarity problem (FDA, 2022) (NICE, 2023).

Take-home message

Speech clarity depends on more than volume. Distance, noise, and signal quality matter.
