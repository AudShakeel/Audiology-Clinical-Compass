Bimodal Listening: Cochlear Implant Plus Hearing Aid

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: CI and hearing aid clinicians

Patient population: Bimodal CI plus contralateral HA users

Clinical purpose: Explain why balance and integration need ongoing care.

Key counseling points

  • Bimodal care aims to combine electric and acoustic input when both ears can provide useful information. Patients may report imbalance, delay, localization difficulty, or different sound quality across ears (Zeitler et al., 2023) (Warner-Czyz et al., 2022).
  • Clinicians should evaluate audibility, comfort, loudness balance, speech perception, device use, earmold acoustics, and communication goals rather than treating bimodal complaints as one setting problem (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (Zeitler et al., 2023).
  • Programming adjustments may involve CI map review, hearing aid verification, gain/output adjustment, frequency lowering review, and counseling about realistic integration (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (Audiology Practice Standards Organization, 2021) (Zeitler et al., 2023).

Clinician notes

Use with bimodal troubleshooting visits and outcome tracking. Avoid manufacturer-specific menu instructions.

Red flags and referral indicators

Facial twitching, new dizziness, pain, device site swelling, or sudden unilateral change should trigger CI team or medical referral (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022) (Warner-Czyz et al., 2022).

What happens next

Track with aided thresholds, speech testing, SSQ, COSI, CIQOL when appropriate, and patient-specific goals.
