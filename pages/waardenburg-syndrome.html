Waardenburg Syndrome

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Waardenburg syndrome is a genetic condition that can involve sensorineural hearing loss and differences in pigmentation or facial features, depending on type.

Patient message: Genetic information can affect the patient and family. Consider genetic counseling or medical genetics referral when recommended by the care team.

Facts at a glance

  • Syndromic genetic hearing loss can include hearing differences plus findings in other body systems (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026).
  • Hearing loss may be congenital and can vary in degree and configuration (Shearer et al., 2025).
  • Early identification and intervention help support language, communication, and development (JCIH, 2019) .

Possible features

  • Sensorineural hearing loss in one or both ears.
  • Differences in eye color, hair pigmentation, or skin pigmentation.
  • Facial feature differences or other findings depending on the type.
  • Family history may or may not be obvious.

Audiology care

  • Diagnostic hearing evaluation.
  • Hearing aid, cochlear implant, or other technology discussion depending on hearing levels and benefit.
  • Speech-language, educational, and family support when needed.

Family and school planning

  • Share hearing access needs with school teams.
  • Use remote microphone systems, seating, and written support when appropriate.
  • Consider genetic counseling for family questions about inheritance and recurrence.

When to seek help

  • New hearing concerns, speech delay, school difficulty, ear symptoms, or sudden change in hearing should be reviewed.

Take-home message

Waardenburg syndrome hearing care should be individualized because hearing levels and related features can vary.
