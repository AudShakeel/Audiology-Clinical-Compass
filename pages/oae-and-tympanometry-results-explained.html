OAE and Tympanometry Results Explained

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Diagnostic audiologists, pediatric clinicians

Patient population: Patients and families reviewing test results

Clinical purpose: Explain common objective tests in simple language.

Key counseling points

  • OAEs assess outer hair cell function and help cross-check cochlear status, but they do not measure the whole auditory system or guarantee normal hearing in every case (American Academy of Audiology, 2020) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019).
  • Tympanometry helps evaluate middle-ear status and can explain conductive components, fluctuating hearing, or test reliability issues (American Academy of Audiology, 2020) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).
  • Audiologists interpret these tests with case history, otoscopy, behavioral results, speech testing, and medical context (American Academy of Audiology, 2020) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).

Clinician notes

Use after diagnostic testing. Avoid implying that one test gives the complete diagnosis.

Red flags and referral indicators

Pain, drainage, sudden hearing loss, abnormal otoscopy, or persistent middle-ear concerns should prompt medical referral (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).

What happens next

Schedule follow-up testing, ENT referral, or amplification planning based on the complete test battery.
