OAE and Tympanometry Results Explained

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Otoacoustic emissions and tympanometry are common tests that provide information about cochlear outer hair cell function and middle-ear status.

Patient message: A pass or present result does not always mean hearing is normal, and an absent result does not always mean permanent hearing loss. Results must be interpreted together.

Facts at a glance

  • Pediatric and adult assessment may combine multiple tests because no single test explains every part of hearing (American Academy of Audiology, 2020) (NICE, 2023).
  • OAEs can be affected by cochlear status, middle-ear function, ear canal blockage, and test conditions (American Academy of Audiology, 2020).
  • Tympanometry helps evaluate eardrum and middle-ear function but does not directly measure speech understanding (American Academy of Audiology, 2020) (FDA, 2022) .

OAE basics

  • A small probe plays sounds and measures an echo-like response from the cochlea.
  • OAEs are often used in newborn screening and diagnostic test batteries.
  • Middle-ear fluid or blockage can prevent OAEs from being measured even if the cochlea is functioning.

Tympanometry basics

  • A probe changes air pressure in the ear canal to see how the eardrum moves.
  • Results may suggest normal mobility, reduced mobility, negative pressure, perforation pattern, or other middle-ear concerns.
  • Tympanometry does not tell the whole hearing picture by itself.

Why results may not match

  • Middle-ear problems can affect OAE and audiogram results.
  • Auditory neuropathy concerns may show present OAEs but abnormal neural responses.
  • Ear canal seal, noise, movement, and probe fit can affect test quality.

Questions to ask

  • Do the results suggest outer, middle, inner-ear, or neural concerns?
  • Is follow-up testing needed?
  • Should medical evaluation be considered?

Take-home message

OAE and tympanometry are pieces of the puzzle, not the whole hearing diagnosis.
