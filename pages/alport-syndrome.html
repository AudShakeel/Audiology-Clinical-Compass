Alport Syndrome

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Alport syndrome is a genetic condition that can affect kidneys, hearing, and sometimes eyes. Hearing loss is usually sensorineural and may develop over time.

Patient message: Genetic information can affect the patient and family. Consider genetic counseling or medical genetics referral when recommended by the care team.

Facts at a glance

  • Genetic hearing loss can be syndromic and connected with kidney, vision, or other medical findings (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026).
  • Hearing health is important for communication, participation, education, and quality of life (WHO, 2021).
  • Patients with syndromic hearing loss need coordinated medical and audiologic follow-up (Shearer et al., 2025) .

Hearing features

  • Hearing loss may not be present at birth.
  • It may develop or progress during childhood, adolescence, or adulthood depending on the person.
  • The loss is commonly sensorineural.

Other health features

  • Kidney monitoring is central to medical care.
  • Eye findings may occur in some patients.
  • Family history and inheritance questions should be discussed with genetics or the medical team.

Audiology care

  • Baseline hearing test when recommended.
  • Regular monitoring if risk is known.
  • Hearing aids or other technology when communication is affected.
  • Communication support at school, work, and home.

When to seek help

  • Sudden hearing change, new tinnitus, difficulty understanding speech, school or work communication concerns, or medical changes should be discussed with the care team.

Take-home message

Alport syndrome hearing care focuses on monitoring and timely communication support.
