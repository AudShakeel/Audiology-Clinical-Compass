Cochlear Implant Activation Expectations

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: CI audiologists and counseling clinicians

Patient population: New cochlear implant recipients and families

Clinical purpose: Prepare patients for early sound quality and follow-up needs.

Key counseling points

  • CI activation is the beginning of rehabilitation, not the end of treatment. Sound may initially seem mechanical, unclear, high-pitched, or unfamiliar as the brain learns to use the input (Warner-Czyz et al., 2022) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2019).
  • Progress depends on hearing history, duration of deafness, device use, mapping, auditory practice, communication needs, and coexisting medical or developmental factors (Warner-Czyz et al., 2022) (Zeitler et al., 2023).
  • Follow-up programming, auditory rehabilitation, outcome measurement, and communication support are part of responsible CI care (Warner-Czyz et al., 2022) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2019).

Clinician notes

Use before activation and at early follow-up. Avoid promising speech outcomes. Emphasize measurable goals and daily use.

Red flags and referral indicators

Facial stimulation, persistent pain, swelling, infection signs, new severe dizziness, sudden device failure, or neurologic symptoms require prompt CI team or medical review (Warner-Czyz et al., 2022) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2019).

What happens next

Coordinate with CI center, SLP, ENT, school team, and manufacturer support only for device-specific technical issues.

References used for this handout
