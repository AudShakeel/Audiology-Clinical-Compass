Meniere Disease: Counseling Overview

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Meniere disease is an inner-ear disorder associated with episodes of vertigo and fluctuating auditory symptoms such as hearing change, tinnitus, or ear fullness.

Patient message: Meniere symptoms can fluctuate. Bring a symptom diary if possible.

Facts at a glance

  • The clinical guideline focuses on suspected definite or probable Meniere disease in adults and improving diagnosis, treatment, and outcome monitoring (AAO-HNSF, 2020).
  • Hearing loss, tinnitus, vertigo, and aural fullness can affect quality of life and should be tracked over time (AAO-HNSF, 2020) (NICE, 2020).
  • Audiology contributes by monitoring hearing, speech understanding, hearing technology needs, tinnitus concerns, and functional impact (AAO-HNSF, 2020) (NICE, 2023) .

Common features

  • Vertigo episodes.
  • Fluctuating hearing, often worse during or around episodes.
  • Tinnitus.
  • Aural fullness or pressure.
  • Fatigue or imbalance after attacks.

What to track

  • Episode date, duration, severity, and triggers.
  • Which ear feels affected.
  • Hearing changes before, during, or after episodes.
  • Tinnitus changes, fullness, nausea, falls, and medication use.

Audiology care may include

  • Serial audiograms.
  • Speech understanding testing.
  • Hearing aid or assistive technology discussion when hearing loss affects communication.
  • Tinnitus counseling or referral.
  • Outcome tracking for vertigo, hearing, tinnitus, and quality of life (AAO-HNSF, 2020).

Urgent symptoms

  • Sudden hearing loss, neurologic symptoms, severe headache, inability to walk safely, new continuous vertigo, or chest pain should be medically evaluated.

Take-home message

Meniere care is coordinated. Audiology helps track hearing and communication needs over time.
