Meniere Disease: Counseling Overview

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Vestibular audiologists, ENT teams

Patient population: Patients with episodic vertigo, fluctuating hearing, tinnitus, or aural fullness

Clinical purpose: Provide a general information sheet without replacing ENT care.

Original patient counseling visual. It does not replace vestibular testing or medical evaluation.

Key counseling points

  • Meniere disease is a clinical diagnosis that typically involves episodic vertigo with fluctuating auditory symptoms such as hearing change, tinnitus, or aural fullness (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2020).
  • Audiology contributes by documenting hearing patterns, speech understanding, vestibular findings when appropriate, functional impact, and communication needs (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2020).
  • Counseling should emphasize symptom tracking, fall safety, hearing monitoring, tinnitus support, and coordinated medical management (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2020) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2020).

Clinician notes

Use with ENT collaboration. Avoid presenting diet, medication, or procedure guidance as audiology-directed treatment.

Red flags and referral indicators

Emergency or medical referral is needed for neurologic symptoms, sudden hearing loss, continuous severe vertigo, severe headache, or inability to walk safely (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2020) (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

What happens next

Consider DHI, HHIA/HHIE, tinnitus measure link, audiogram monitoring, and ENT follow-up.
