Communication Strategies for Families

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Communication improves when the speaker and listener work together. Families can make small changes that reduce listening effort and misunderstanding.

Patient message: The goal is not to blame the speaker or the listener. The goal is to make the message easier to access.

Facts at a glance

  • Adult aural rehabilitation includes counseling, communication strategies, assistive technology, and patient-centered goals (ASHA, 2024).
  • Hearing technology often helps, but communication challenges may remain in noise or at a distance (ASHA, 2024) (NICE, 2023).
  • Family involvement can support daily participation and realistic expectations (WHO, 2021) (ASHA, 2024) .

Helpful speaker habits

  • Face the listener before speaking.
  • Get attention first by saying the person name or using a gentle cue.
  • Speak clearly at a natural pace. Do not shout from another room.
  • Reduce background noise when the message is important (ASHA, 2024) (NICE, 2023).

Helpful listener habits

  • Tell others what helps, such as facing you or moving closer.
  • Repeat back the part you heard and ask for the missing piece.
  • Use captions, written notes, phone transcription, or a remote microphone when appropriate.
  • Track situations that remain hard so your audiologist can adjust the care plan (ASHA, 2024).

Home and social settings

  • Sit where you can see faces.
  • Choose smaller groups or quieter times when possible.
  • Turn off competing sound during important conversations.
  • Consider assistive listening technology when distance is the main problem (American Academy of Audiology, 2008).

When to seek help

  • If communication suddenly worsens, one ear changes, dizziness starts, or hearing devices no longer help, contact your audiologist or medical provider as advised (FDA, 2022) (NICE, 2023).

Take-home message

Good communication is a shared task. Small environmental changes can make a large difference.
