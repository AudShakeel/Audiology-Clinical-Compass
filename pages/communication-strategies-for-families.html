Communication Strategies for Families

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Patients, family members, caregivers

Patient population: Adults or children with communication difficulty

Clinical purpose: Give family members specific behaviors that improve communication.

Key counseling points

  • Clear communication is a shared responsibility. Face the listener, reduce competing noise, speak clearly, and check understanding without shouting (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).
  • Distance matters. Moving closer and improving visual access often helps more than raising volume across a room (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (American Academy of Audiology, 2008).
  • For important conversations, repeat using different words, write key details, and confirm dates, medications, and safety instructions (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (World Health Organization, 2021).

Clinician notes

Print for family counseling and aural rehabilitation. Ask the patient to choose two strategies to practice this week.

Red flags and referral indicators

Refer for acute medical red flags or when communication changes are sudden, asymmetric, or associated with neurologic symptoms (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

What happens next

Consider remote microphones, captioned phone, written summaries, and workplace or classroom accommodations.
