CHD7 Disorder and CHARGE Features

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Audiologists, families, students

Patient population: Patients with diagnosed or suspected genetic, syndromic, or congenital hearing and balance concerns

Clinical purpose: Provide a concise patient education handout for CHD7 Disorder and CHARGE Features.

Key counseling points

  • CHD7-related disorder can affect multiple systems, including hearing, balance, vision, airway, feeding, growth, and development. (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019) (American Academy of Audiology, 2013).
  • Hearing loss can be conductive, sensorineural, or mixed. Middle-ear disease and ear malformations can complicate management. (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019) (American Academy of Audiology, 2013).
  • Care usually requires coordinated medical, audiology, speech-language, educational, and developmental services. (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019) (American Academy of Audiology, 2013).

Clinician notes

Use as a starting education sheet only. Confirm diagnosis-specific counseling with medical genetics, ENT, and the responsible medical team. Avoid implying that all patients with the syndrome have the same course.

Red flags and referral indicators

Red flags include sudden hearing change, severe dizziness, neurologic symptoms, cardiac symptoms, vision concerns, kidney concerns, developmental regression, or any acute medical symptom. Refer based on diagnosis and local protocol (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022) (Shearer et al., 2025).

What happens next

Document diagnosis source, family questions, communication plan, device plan, referral status, and follow-up interval.
