CHD7 Disorder and CHARGE Features

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: CHD7 disorder, often associated with CHARGE features, can involve hearing loss, balance differences, cranial nerve findings, ear differences, and complex medical needs.

Patient message: Genetic information can affect the patient and family. Consider genetic counseling or medical genetics referral when recommended by the care team.

Facts at a glance

  • Syndromic genetic hearing loss may involve multiple body systems and requires coordinated care (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026).
  • Pediatric amplification and assessment should consider safety, anatomy, development, and family-centered goals (American Academy of Audiology, 2013) (American Academy of Audiology, 2020).
  • Early intervention and communication planning are important for children who are deaf or hard of hearing (JCIH, 2019) .

Possible hearing and balance features

  • Conductive, sensorineural, or mixed hearing loss.
  • External, middle, or inner-ear differences.
  • Balance delay or vestibular hypofunction.
  • Feeding, vision, airway, cardiac, or developmental concerns may also affect care.

Audiology care

  • Use a test battery matched to age, medical status, and tolerance.
  • Consider bone-conduction, air-conduction, cochlear implant, or other options based on findings.
  • Coordinate with ENT, genetics, ophthalmology, therapy, school, and medical teams.

Family priorities

  • Communication access in daily routines.
  • Safe device retention and comfort.
  • Monitoring for ear disease and device tolerance.
  • Developmental support and school planning.

When to seek help

  • New hearing concerns, device intolerance, ear drainage, new imbalance, feeding or airway changes, or medical instability should be reported to the appropriate care team.

Take-home message

CHD7-related care should be individualized because hearing, balance, medical, and developmental needs can be complex.
