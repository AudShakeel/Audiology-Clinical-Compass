Pediatric Hearing Aid Parent Counseling

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Pediatric audiologists, parents, caregivers

Patient population: Infants and children using hearing aids

Clinical purpose: Give parents a clear daily-use roadmap.

Key counseling points

  • Pediatric amplification should be fit, verified, and monitored using child-specific ear acoustics, developmental needs, family goals, and ongoing outcome measures (American Academy of Audiology, 2013) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019).
  • Consistent access to sound supports communication development, but families need practical help with retention, safety, feedback, earmold changes, moisture, and routines (American Academy of Audiology, 2013) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019).
  • Children need repeated follow-up because ears grow, hearing may change, earmolds become loose, and listening needs change across home, early intervention, and school settings (American Academy of Audiology, 2013) (American Academy of Audiology, 2020) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019).

Clinician notes

Use at fitting, earmold visits, EI meetings, school-transition visits, and inconsistent-wear counseling.

Red flags and referral indicators

Medical referral is needed for drainage, pain, sudden change, recurrent abnormal middle ear findings, suspected infection, or syndromic concerns (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019) (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

What happens next

Use LittlEARS, PEACH, TEACH, IT-MAIS/MAIS, CHILD, LIFE-R, SIFTER, or age-appropriate goals by official source availability.

References used for this handout

  1. Joint Committee on Infant Hearing. (2019). Principles and guidelines for early hearing detection and intervention programs. Official source. Consensus guideline
  2. American Academy of Audiology. (2013). Clinical practice guidelines: Pediatric amplification. Official source. Clinical guideline
  3. American Academy of Audiology. (2020). Assessment of hearing in infants and young children. Official source. Clinical guidance
