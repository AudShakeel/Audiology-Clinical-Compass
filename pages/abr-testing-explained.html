ABR Testing Explained

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Auditory brainstem response testing measures how the hearing nerve and brainstem respond to sound. It may be used when behavioral testing is not enough or when specific diagnostic questions are present.

Patient message: ABR is a test of auditory pathway response, not a test of intelligence or attention.

Facts at a glance

  • Infant and young child assessment may combine physiologic and behavioral methods to estimate hearing status accurately (American Academy of Audiology, 2020) (JCIH, 2019).
  • ABR can support diagnosis and monitoring but must be interpreted with the full clinical picture (American Academy of Audiology, 2020).
  • Early identification and follow-up are important for children who are deaf or hard of hearing (JCIH, 2019) .

Why ABR may be recommended

  • A baby did not pass newborn hearing screening.
  • Behavioral responses are not yet reliable.
  • Auditory neuropathy spectrum disorder is a concern.
  • Medical history or risk factors require more information.

What happens during the test

  • Small sensors are placed on the head or skin.
  • Sounds are played through earphones or inserts.
  • The child may need to sleep naturally or have medically supervised sedation depending on age, clinic protocol, and test needs.
  • The audiologist looks for repeatable response patterns.

What results may show

  • Estimated hearing levels by frequency or click stimulus.
  • Neural timing information.
  • Whether more testing, monitoring, amplification, or referral is needed.
  • Results may be combined with OAE, tympanometry, acoustic reflexes, and behavioral testing (American Academy of Audiology, 2020).

Questions to ask

  • Is the test diagnostic or screening?
  • Were both ears tested?
  • Are middle-ear problems affecting the results?
  • What is the follow-up timeline?

Take-home message

ABR helps estimate hearing and auditory pathway function when behavioral testing is limited or diagnostic detail is needed.
