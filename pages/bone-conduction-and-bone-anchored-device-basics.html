Bone-Conduction and Bone-Anchored Device Basics

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Audiologists, trainees, families

Patient population: Conductive, mixed, SSD, atresia, and selected medical cases

Clinical purpose: Provide neutral counseling without manufacturer instructions.

Key counseling points

  • Bone-conduction devices route sound through skull vibration and may be considered for selected conductive, mixed, single-sided, or anatomical ear canal conditions after appropriate evaluation (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023) (World Health Organization, 2021).
  • Candidacy depends on diagnosis, bone-conduction thresholds, ear anatomy, medical status, age, retention, communication goals, and patient or family preference (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023) (Joint Committee on Infant Hearing, 2019).
  • Patients need counseling on retention, skin care, device orientation, realistic sound quality, and referral pathways for surgical questions (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023) (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

Clinician notes

Use as an introductory handout. Link to official manufacturer pages only when discussing model-specific care.

Red flags and referral indicators

Active drainage, pain, suspected infection, skin breakdown, or sudden change in hearing requires medical review (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

What happens next

Coordinate audiology, ENT, school support, and device-specific manufacturer guidance as needed.
