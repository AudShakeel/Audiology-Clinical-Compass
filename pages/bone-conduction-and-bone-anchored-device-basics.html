Bone-Conduction and Bone-Anchored Device Basics

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Bone-conduction devices send sound vibrations through bone to the inner ear. They may be considered for certain conductive, mixed, or single-sided hearing situations.

Patient message: A bone-conduction device is not the same as a regular air-conduction hearing aid. It sends vibration through bone instead of sending amplified sound through the ear canal.

Facts at a glance

  • Device options depend on hearing-loss type, ear health, anatomy, age, medical candidacy, and patient goals (NICE, 2023) (FDA, 2022).
  • Children need coordinated hearing monitoring, family support, and educational access planning when hearing loss is present (JCIH, 2019).
  • Hearing technology should be matched to the individual rather than selected by diagnosis alone (WHO, 2021) (NICE, 2023) .

Who may be evaluated

  • People who cannot use an ear canal device because of chronic drainage, anatomy, or surgical history.
  • People with conductive or mixed hearing loss when medical and audiologic findings support the option.
  • Some people with single-sided deafness, depending on goals, candidacy, and available options.

What the evaluation may include

  • Audiogram and bone-conduction thresholds.
  • Speech testing, device demonstration, comfort review, and discussion of expectations.
  • Medical evaluation may be needed before implantable or surgical options are considered (FDA, 2022) (NICE, 2023).

What to expect

  • Sound may be clearer than without the device but may not sound identical to natural hearing.
  • Benefit can depend on placement, processor settings, anatomy, hearing in the other ear, and listening environment.
  • Children may need retention, daily device checks, school support, and caregiver training.

When to get help

  • Skin irritation, pressure discomfort, weak sound, feedback, intermittent sound, or reduced daily use should be reviewed.
  • Pain, drainage, sudden hearing change, or dizziness requires medical or audiology guidance (FDA, 2022).

Take-home message

Bone-conduction devices are an option for specific hearing profiles and should be evaluated with both medical and audiologic input.
