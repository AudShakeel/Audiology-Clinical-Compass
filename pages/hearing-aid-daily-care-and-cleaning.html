Hearing Aid Daily Care and Cleaning

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Adults, caregivers, students

Patient population: Hearing aid users across adult clinical settings

Clinical purpose: Support device reliability through safe daily care.

Key counseling points

  • Daily listening checks, clean microphones, open sound ports, dry storage, and correct insertion reduce avoidable weak-sound and intermittency complaints (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).
  • Rechargeable patients should place devices in the charger every night. Battery patients should learn battery direction, door closure, and safe storage away from children and pets (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).
  • Cerumen, moisture, blocked wax filters, collapsed domes, and loose receiver wires are common non-programming causes of poor sound quality (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).

Clinician notes

Use as a printable orientation sheet. Confirm the patient can insert, remove, clean, charge, and identify right versus left.

Red flags and referral indicators

Do not continue device use when there is pain, drainage, sudden change in hearing, ear swelling, suspected infection, or skin breakdown (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

What happens next

Schedule cleaning follow-up as clinically needed and reinforce that repairs should not be attempted with sharp tools or moisture exposure.
