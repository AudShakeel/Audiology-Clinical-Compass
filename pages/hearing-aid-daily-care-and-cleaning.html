Hearing Aid Daily Care and Cleaning

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Daily care helps prevent many avoidable hearing aid problems, including weak sound, intermittent sound, feedback, and moisture-related malfunction.

Patient message: Use the specific cleaning tools provided by your clinic or manufacturer. Do not use water, alcohol, or household cleaners unless your audiologist specifically recommends them for your device part.

Facts at a glance

  • Follow-up care should include review of cleaning, insertion, battery or charging use, comfort, and daily listening problems (NICE, 2023) (ASHA, 2024).
  • Device benefit can be limited by blocked microphones, clogged wax guards, poor earmold fit, moisture, or weak power supply (ASHA, 2024) (APSO, 2021).
  • Do not place tools deep into the ear canal, and seek help if wax, pain, drainage, or sudden hearing change is present (FDA, 2022) .

Daily routine

  • Wash and dry your hands before handling devices.
  • Wipe the devices with a dry tissue or approved cloth after use.
  • Check the microphone ports, receiver opening, earmold, dome, wax guard, and battery or charger connection.
  • Make sure the device is fully seated in the ear because poor insertion can cause weak sound or feedback (ASHA, 2024).

Weekly routine

  • Replace wax guards or domes according to clinic instructions.
  • Brush microphone openings gently with the opening facing downward.
  • Check earmold tubing for cracks, hardening, moisture, or discoloration.
  • Report repeated blockages or feedback because they may indicate wax, fit, or acoustic changes that need clinic review (ASHA, 2024) (FDA, 2022).

What not to do

  • Do not sleep, shower, swim, or use hair products while wearing devices unless your audiologist confirms your device is designed for that use.
  • Do not store devices in a hot car, near pets, or within reach of young children.
  • Do not ignore ear pain, drainage, sudden change, dizziness, or one-sided symptoms (FDA, 2022) (NICE, 2023).

When to call the clinic

  • Sound is weak after changing the battery or charging fully.
  • The device whistles even when inserted correctly.
  • Sound cuts in and out, sounds distorted, or stops after moisture exposure.
  • The receiver, dome, earmold, or battery door is loose or damaged.

Take-home message

Most daily care takes less than two minutes and can prevent many urgent device visits.
