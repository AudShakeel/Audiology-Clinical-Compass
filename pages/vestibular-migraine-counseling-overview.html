Vestibular Migraine: Counseling Overview

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Vestibular audiologists, ENT, neurology partners

Patient population: Patients with recurrent vertigo/dizziness and migraine features

Clinical purpose: Provide neutral education and referral guidance.

Original patient counseling visual. It does not replace vestibular testing or medical evaluation.

Key counseling points

  • Vestibular migraine is diagnosed from a pattern of vestibular episodes, migraine history or features, and exclusion of better explanations (Lempert et al., 2022).
  • Audiologists help by characterizing vestibular symptoms, hearing findings, triggers, functional impact, and need for medical referral rather than diagnosing from dizziness alone (Lempert et al., 2022) (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2020).
  • Counseling often includes symptom diary, sleep and hydration discussion, trigger awareness, fall precautions, and referral to medical clinicians for migraine management (Lempert et al., 2022).

Clinician notes

Use when audiology findings and history suggest migraine-related dizziness. Coordinate with ENT or neurology.

Red flags and referral indicators

Refer urgently for acute neurologic symptoms, severe new headache, fainting, chest pain, new unilateral hearing loss, or continuous acute vestibular syndrome (Lempert et al., 2022) (American Academy of Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery Foundation, 2017).

What happens next

Consider DHI, ABC, VAP, symptom diary, and vestibular rehab referral if medically appropriate.
