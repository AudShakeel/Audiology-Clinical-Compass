Tinnitus Counseling and Red Flags

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Tinnitus is the perception of sound when no outside sound is present. It may be described as ringing, buzzing, hissing, roaring, clicking, or tones.

Patient message: Tinnitus is real. The goal of counseling is not to prove it is imaginary. The goal is to reduce distress, improve function, and identify signs that need medical care.

Facts at a glance

  • Tinnitus management should include targeted history, hearing evaluation when appropriate, education, and referral for concerning features (NICE, 2020) (AAO-HNSF, 2014).
  • Persistent bothersome tinnitus may be helped by counseling, hearing care, sound strategies, and support for sleep, stress, and attention (AAO-HNSF, 2014) (NCRAR, 2024).
  • Tinnitus with sudden hearing change, pulsatile sound, neurologic signs, or one-sided concerning symptoms needs medical review (NICE, 2020) (AAO-HNSF, 2014) .

What can make tinnitus more noticeable

  • Quiet rooms, fatigue, stress, poor sleep, anxiety, and attention to the sound.
  • Hearing loss or reduced sound input.
  • Certain medical or medication factors that should be reviewed by the appropriate medical professional.

What can help

  • Education and counseling can reduce fear and improve coping (AAO-HNSF, 2014) (NCRAR, 2024).
  • Soft background sound can reduce contrast between tinnitus and silence.
  • Hearing aids may help some patients when tinnitus occurs with hearing loss.
  • Sleep routines, stress support, and mental health referral may be appropriate when tinnitus causes significant distress.

Red flags

  • Seek medical guidance for sudden hearing loss, pulsatile tinnitus, one-sided tinnitus with asymmetric hearing concerns, ear pain or drainage, dizziness, neurologic symptoms, or severe distress (NICE, 2020) (AAO-HNSF, 2014).

Questions to ask

  • Is my tinnitus associated with hearing loss?
  • Do any features suggest medical referral?
  • Which sound strategies are safe for me?
  • Should I use an outcome measure such as THI, TFI, THS, or another tool from an official source?

Take-home message

Tinnitus care should be calm, structured, and focused on function, safety, and distress reduction.
