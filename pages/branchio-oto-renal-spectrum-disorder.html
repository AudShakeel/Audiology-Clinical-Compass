Branchio-Oto-Renal Spectrum Disorder

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Branchio-oto-renal spectrum disorder can involve ear differences, hearing loss, branchial cleft findings, and kidney differences.

Patient message: Genetic information can affect the patient and family. Consider genetic counseling or medical genetics referral when recommended by the care team.

Facts at a glance

  • Syndromic genetic hearing loss may involve other organ systems, so audiology findings can be part of a broader medical picture (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026).
  • Hearing loss can be conductive, sensorineural, or mixed depending on ear anatomy and other findings (Shearer et al., 2025).
  • Children with hearing loss need timely communication access and monitoring (JCIH, 2019) .

Possible features

  • Outer ear differences, ear pits, tags, or canal differences.
  • Conductive, sensorineural, or mixed hearing loss.
  • Branchial cleft cysts or fistulas.
  • Kidney or urinary tract differences that require medical care.

Audiology care

  • Baseline and follow-up hearing tests.
  • Middle-ear and ear canal assessment.
  • Hearing technology planning, which may include air-conduction, bone-conduction, or implantable options depending on findings.
  • Educational and speech-language support when needed.

Coordinated care

  • ENT, nephrology, genetics, pediatrics, and school teams may all be involved.
  • Bring imaging, operative notes, genetic reports, and prior audiograms to appointments.

When to seek help

  • Drainage, ear pain, sudden hearing change, device fit problems, balance concerns, or changes in kidney-related health should be reported to the appropriate clinician.

Take-home message

BOR spectrum care works best when ear, hearing, kidney, and family information are coordinated.
