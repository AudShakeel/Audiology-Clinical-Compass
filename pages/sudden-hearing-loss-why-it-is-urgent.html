Sudden Hearing Loss: Why It Is Urgent

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Audiologists, front desk triage, trainees

Patient population: Patients reporting sudden or rapidly progressive hearing change

Clinical purpose: Provide clear referral language.

Key counseling points

  • Sudden or rapidly progressive hearing change should not be treated as routine wax, aging, or hearing aid malfunction until appropriate medical evaluation is considered (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).
  • Unilateral sudden change, sudden tinnitus with hearing change, dizziness, ear pain, drainage, or neurologic symptoms increases concern and should prompt urgent referral based on local protocol (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).
  • Audiology can document history, otoscopy, audiometric findings, and referral communication, but treatment timing is medical (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).

Clinician notes

Use for phone triage scripts and patient handouts. Include your clinic emergency referral process locally.

Red flags and referral indicators

Same-day or urgent care may be indicated for sudden hearing loss, neurologic signs, severe dizziness, facial weakness, severe headache, or trauma (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).

What happens next

Document onset time, ear, associated symptoms, referral instructions, and patient understanding.
