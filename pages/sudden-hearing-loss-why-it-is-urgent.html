Sudden Hearing Loss: Why It Is Urgent

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Sudden hearing loss is a medical urgency. Do not wait to see if it improves on its own.

Patient message: If one ear suddenly feels blocked, muffled, or distorted, it may be hearing loss, not just wax or congestion.

Facts at a glance

  • Sudden or rapidly progressive hearing loss should be referred urgently according to clinical referral guidance (NICE, 2023).
  • Medical warning signs for hearing aid users include sudden hearing change, ear pain, drainage, acute dizziness, and other ear-related concerns (FDA, 2022).
  • Prompt evaluation is important because treatment options may be time-sensitive depending on the cause (NICE, 2023) .

Symptoms that need urgent attention

  • Sudden hearing loss in one or both ears.
  • Rapidly worsening hearing over hours or days.
  • New one-sided tinnitus with hearing change.
  • New dizziness or vertigo with hearing change.
  • Ear pain, drainage, trauma, or neurologic symptoms.

What to do

  • Contact ENT, urgent care, emergency services, or the pathway your clinic recommends.
  • Tell the office exactly when the hearing change started.
  • Do not wait for a routine hearing aid appointment if the hearing change is sudden.

What audiology may do

  • Document hearing levels and speech understanding.
  • Compare with prior audiograms.
  • Check for ear canal obstruction if appropriate.
  • Coordinate urgent medical referral when indicated.

Questions to ask

  • Is this sudden sensorineural hearing loss possible?
  • How soon should I be seen?
  • Should my hearing aid or cochlear implant map be changed now or after medical evaluation?

Take-home message

Sudden hearing change should be treated as urgent until a qualified medical professional says otherwise.
