Restaurant and Group Conversation Tips

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Restaurants and group conversations are hard because speech competes with noise, music, distance, echo, and multiple talkers.

Patient message: This is not a failure by the listener. It is a difficult acoustic setting.

Facts at a glance

  • Noise and reverberation reduce access to speech and may remain challenging even with hearing aids (ASHA, 2024) (NICE, 2023).
  • Speech-in-noise measures can help document difficulty and guide counseling or technology choices (Etymotic Research, 2024) (ASHA, 2024).
  • Remote microphone systems can improve access by placing the microphone closer to the talker (American Academy of Audiology, 2008) .

Before you go

  • Choose a quieter restaurant, earlier reservation time, or table away from kitchen, bar, speakers, and large parties.
  • Ask for a booth or wall behind you to reduce competing sound from behind.
  • Preview menus online so less listening is needed in noise.

During conversation

  • Sit where you can see faces and speech cues.
  • Ask one person to speak at a time for important details.
  • Use a remote microphone, table microphone mode, or phone captioning app if appropriate.
  • Try a noise program or directional microphone setting when your devices support it (ASHA, 2024) (American Academy of Audiology, 2008).

After the appointment

  • Tell your audiologist which settings were hardest.
  • Ask whether the noise program, low-frequency gain, directional settings, compression, or remote microphone options should be reviewed.
  • Consider speech-in-noise testing if the difficulty is significant despite routine audiogram results (Etymotic Research, 2024).

When to seek help

  • A sudden new change, dizziness, ear pain, drainage, or one-sided symptoms should be reported rather than treated as only a restaurant problem (FDA, 2022).

Take-home message

Better seating, clearer speaker habits, and the right technology can reduce listening effort in groups.
