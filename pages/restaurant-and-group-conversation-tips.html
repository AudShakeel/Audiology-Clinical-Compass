Restaurant and Group Conversation Tips

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Adults and families

Patient population: Patients with speech-in-noise difficulty

Clinical purpose: Provide practical strategies for noisy social settings.

Key counseling points

  • Noise, distance, multiple talkers, and reverberation make speech harder to follow even with well-fit devices (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024).
  • Choose quieter seating, sit with the main talker in front, avoid sitting near kitchens or speakers, and ask communication partners to speak one at a time (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, 2023).
  • Remote microphones and directional programs may improve the signal reaching the listener, but they require orientation and practice (American Academy of Audiology, 2008) (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024).

Clinician notes

Use when a patient reports restaurants are impossible. Pair with QuickSIN, SSQ, COSI, or APHAB if available.

Red flags and referral indicators

A sudden new inability to understand, especially if unilateral or paired with dizziness, tinnitus, or neurologic symptoms, should prompt medical referral (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

What happens next

Try a restaurant program, remote microphone, placement counseling, and follow-up goal tracking.
