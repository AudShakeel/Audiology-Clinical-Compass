Jervell and Lange-Nielsen Syndrome

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Jervell and Lange-Nielsen syndrome is a rare genetic condition associated with congenital sensorineural hearing loss and a heart rhythm condition called long QT syndrome.

Patient message: This condition requires medical coordination. Audiology care should not be separated from cardiology and genetics guidance.

Facts at a glance

  • Some syndromic genetic hearing losses include medical findings that can be serious outside the ear (Shearer et al., 2025) (GeneReviews, 2026).
  • Children with congenital hearing loss need timely hearing and communication support (JCIH, 2019).
  • Medical management of heart rhythm concerns must be directed by cardiology or the treating medical team (GeneReviews, 2026) .

Hearing features

  • Hearing loss is typically congenital and sensorineural.
  • Degree may be severe to profound.
  • Hearing aids, cochlear implant referral, or other technology may be discussed depending on results and benefit.

Medical features

  • Long QT syndrome can affect heart rhythm.
  • Symptoms such as fainting, seizures, or sudden collapse require urgent medical attention.
  • Medication and activity guidance must come from cardiology or the medical team.

Audiology care

  • Early diagnostic evaluation.
  • Rapid communication access planning.
  • Coordination with pediatric, cardiology, genetics, ENT, speech-language, and school teams.
  • Family education about device use and emergency planning.

When to seek help

  • Fainting, seizure-like episodes, chest symptoms, sudden collapse, sudden hearing change, or device non-use should be reported promptly to the proper team.

Take-home message

This condition combines hearing care with potentially serious cardiac care, so coordination is essential.
