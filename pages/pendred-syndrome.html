Pendred Syndrome

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: Pendred syndrome is a genetic condition associated with early hearing loss, thyroid involvement, and sometimes balance concerns.

Patient message: Genetic information can affect the patient and family. Consider genetic counseling or medical genetics referral when recommended by the care team.

Facts at a glance

  • NIDCD describes Pendred syndrome as a genetic disorder that causes early hearing loss in children and can affect the thyroid gland and sometimes balance (NIDCD, 2017).
  • Enlarged vestibular aqueduct may be associated with childhood hearing loss and can be connected with SLC26A4-related conditions (NIDCD, 2017) (Shearer et al., 2025).
  • Children with hearing loss need timely hearing care and intervention planning (JCIH, 2019) .

Hearing and balance features

  • Hearing loss is often sensorineural and may be present early in childhood.
  • Hearing levels can fluctuate or progress.
  • Some patients have enlarged vestibular aqueduct or inner-ear differences.
  • Balance concerns can occur in some patients.

Other health features

  • Thyroid enlargement or other thyroid concerns may occur and should be managed by medical professionals.
  • Genetic testing and imaging may be discussed by the medical team when clinically appropriate.

Audiology care

  • Baseline and repeated audiograms.
  • Hearing aid, cochlear implant, or other technology discussion depending on hearing levels and benefit.
  • School and family communication support.
  • Monitoring for changes after head injury or sudden hearing change.

When to seek help

  • Sudden hearing change, new dizziness, head trauma, ear pain, drainage, or school communication changes should be reported promptly.

Take-home message

Pendred syndrome care is coordinated across audiology, ENT, genetics, endocrinology, family, and school teams.
