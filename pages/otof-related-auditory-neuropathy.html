OTOF-Related Auditory Neuropathy

Original patient education handoutPrintable PDFLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

What this handout explains: OTOF-related hearing loss is associated with auditory neuropathy spectrum disorder patterns in which the inner ear may detect sound but neural signaling is not synchronized well enough for speech understanding.

Patient message: A child may appear to hear some sounds but still have very poor speech understanding. This can be confusing for families.

Facts at a glance

  • OTOF-related hearing loss is described in GeneReviews as a genetic form of hearing loss that can present with auditory neuropathy features (Azaiez et al., 2025) (Shearer et al., 2025).
  • Children with auditory access concerns need timely diagnosis, intervention, and communication planning (JCIH, 2019).
  • Pediatric cochlear implant candidacy guidance considers children who receive limited benefit from appropriately fit hearing technology (Warner-Czyz et al., 2022) .

Common test pattern

  • OAEs or cochlear microphonic may be present.
  • ABR may be absent or abnormal.
  • Behavioral responses and speech understanding may not match what families expect from basic detection.
  • Results must be interpreted by the audiology and medical team.

Audiology care

  • Confirm results with a complete test battery.
  • Monitor behavioral hearing and speech understanding.
  • Discuss amplification, cochlear implant referral, and communication options based on the full profile.
  • Coordinate with ENT, genetics, early intervention, speech-language, and school teams.

Family support

  • Track responses to voices, environmental sounds, and daily routines.
  • Use visual communication supports while hearing access is being clarified.
  • Ask for written next steps and timelines.

When to seek help

  • Missed developmental milestones, device non-benefit, sudden change, dizziness, or uncertainty about results should be reviewed promptly.

Take-home message

OTOF-related auditory neuropathy needs careful testing and timely communication access planning.
