CAPD and Listening Difficulty

Original counseling handoutPermission: host original textLast reviewed: May 11, 2026

Intended audience: Audiologists, educational teams, families

Patient population: Children age 7 and older when appropriate and selected adults

Clinical purpose: Explain auditory processing evaluation and intervention boundaries.

Key counseling points

  • CAPD evaluation should consider case history, cognition, language, attention, academic concerns, peripheral hearing, and appropriate test interpretation (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (American Academy of Audiology, 2010).
  • CAPD is not diagnosed from one complaint or one screening task. Differential diagnosis matters because listening difficulty may reflect hearing loss, attention, language, learning, autism, fatigue, or classroom acoustics (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (American Academy of Audiology, 2010).
  • Management may include environmental modifications, remote microphone technology, direct skills work when appropriate, compensatory strategies, and interdisciplinary educational planning (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (American Academy of Audiology, 2010) (American Academy of Audiology, 2008).

Clinician notes

Use as a family and school handout. Avoid copying proprietary test items.

Red flags and referral indicators

Refer when symptoms are sudden, neurologic, medically concerning, or better explained by untreated peripheral hearing loss (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, 2024) (U.S. Food and Drug Administration, 2022).

What happens next

Coordinate with SLP, psychology, school teams, occupational therapy, and medical providers as needed.
